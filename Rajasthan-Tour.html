When visiting Rajasthan, it‚Äôs important to pack with the climate and your planned activities in mind. The state experiences diverse weather, so being prepared for the season makes your journey more comfortable. During summers (April to June), Rajasthan can get extremely hot, especially in desert cities like Jaisalmer and Bikaner. Pack lightweight cotton or linen clothes in light colors to keep cool, along with sunglasses, a wide-brimmed hat, and high-SPF sunscreen to protect yourself from the intense sun. Staying hydrated and wearing breathable fabrics is key. In the monsoon season (July to September), although rainfall is not very heavy, occasional showers do occur. Carry a compact umbrella or raincoat, waterproof pouches for electronics and documents, and shoes with good grip for walking around forts or streets that might be slippery. Winters (October to February) are quite pleasant and sometimes chilly, especially in cities like Jaipur, Udaipur, Jaisalmer, and Mount Abu. Early mornings and evenings can be cold, so include a warm jacket, a shawl, or a pullover, especially if you plan on camping in the desert.
