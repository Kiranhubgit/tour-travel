Why Visit Kerala?

Kerala is a tropical paradise known as “God’s Own Country.” Whether you're seeking serene backwaters, lush green hills, rich culture, or rejuvenating wellness experiences, Kerala has something to offer for every kind of traveler.

Breathtaking Natural Beauty

From the palm-lined beaches of Kovalam and Varkala to the misty hills of Munnar and Wayanad, Kerala's landscapes offer peace and awe-inspiring views for nature lovers and photographers.

Rich Culture

Witness colorful Kathakali performances, explore ancient temples, and experience Onam celebrations that reflect Kerala’s deep-rooted traditions and welcoming spirit.

Historic Wonders

Discover the colonial charm of Fort Kochi, the architectural beauty of Padmanabhaswamy Temple, and centuries-old spice routes that shaped history.

Adventure & Wellness

Enjoy houseboat cruises in Alleppey, trekking in Thekkady, or unwind with traditional Ayurvedic therapies—Kerala is the perfect blend of thrill and relaxation.

Kerala Tour Packages

Kerala is well-connected by air, rail, and road, making it easily accessible from all parts of India. The state has four major international airports — in Thiruvananthapuram, Kochi, Kozhikode, and Kannur — with regular flights from major Indian cities and international destinations. For train travelers, Kerala has an extensive railway network with stations in all major towns like Ernakulam, Trivandrum, and Alappuzha. If you prefer road travel, Kerala is connected via national highways and interstate buses from neighboring states like Karnataka and Tamil Nadu. Comfortable private cabs and KSRTC buses make road journeys scenic and pleasant.

When visiting Kerala, pack according to the season and the activities you plan to enjoy. Lightweight, breathable cotton or linen clothes are ideal year-round due to the warm and humid tropical climate. If you're traveling during the monsoon season (June to September), be sure to carry a sturdy umbrella, raincoat, and waterproof footwear to stay dry during sudden showers, especially if you're exploring areas like Alleppey or Wayanad. For cooler regions such as Munnar, Thekkady, or Vagamon, a light sweater or jacket will keep you comfortable in the evenings. Comfortable walking shoes or sandals are a must for sightseeing, trekking, or nature walks. Also pack sunscreen, insect repellent, sunglasses, and a wide-brimmed hat to protect yourself from the sun. If you plan to visit temples or cultural sites, carry modest clothing (like full-sleeve tops and long pants or skirts) as a sign of respect. Don’t forget personal medications, a reusable water bottle, a power bank, and a camera or phone with enough storage to capture the scenic backwaters, tea gardens, and wildlife. A small daypack can also be useful for day trips and hikes.
